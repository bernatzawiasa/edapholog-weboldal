Why soil biodiversity matters

Soil organisms respond rapidly to changes in land use and management, making biodiversity a reliable early indicator of soil health and humus dynamics. High species richness supports better soil structure, water retention and nutrient cycling — key factors for long-term fertility and climate resilience. Edapholog® focuses on quantifying these signals to inform research and practice.

Standardised protocol & objective evaluation

Edapholog® applies a standardised, reproducible sampling protocol. AI-based image analysis ensures consistent, objective identification and counting, while biostatistical methods enable robust comparisons across sites and over time. The outcome is decision-grade data for monitoring programmes and trials.

Carbon programmes — measurable impact

Biodiversity measured before and after interventions helps verify ecological benefits within carbon and regenerative farming programmes. Edapholog® supports MRV (Monitoring, Reporting, Verification) by delivering auditable indicators that capture changes in soil-life communities and link them to management outcomes.
